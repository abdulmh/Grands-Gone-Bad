Signs & Symptoms

Upset Stomach: Gluten can trigger an immune reaction, creating inflammation that damages the small intestine's lining and leading to medical complications. It also prevents the absorption of some nutrients (malabsorption) and can cause nausea, vomiting, bloating, and cramps. Constipation: Making too many or too little trips to the restroom? Gluten may be blocking your bowels! Individuals with gluten intolerance or celiac disease may experience inflammation in the small intestine after eating the protein, causing constipation and in some cases diarrhea. Acne and hives: Gluten-sensitive people cannot properly digest the protein. Instead, these large molecules enter the bloodstream sending the immune system into defense mode, increasing inflammation, which in turn can result in acne or hives. Cutting gluten out of your diet can help improve those pesky pimples and other skin conditions. Insomnia: Feeling restless? A 2010 study from The University of Naples found that insomnia and sleep problems appear to be a direct symptom of celiac disease. Further research is needed to fully understand the connection between gluten and insufficient sleep. However, recent studies show that acid reflux, headaches, joint pain, anemia, anxiety and depression can persist long after following a gluten free diet.
